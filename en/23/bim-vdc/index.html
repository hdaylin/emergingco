BIM-VDC

An avant-garde collaborative method of work, based on a virtual digital model which provides thorough information about the building or infrastructure, its performance, its constructability, and its operation, among other aspects.

Building Information Modeling & Virtual Design and Construction

To advance this field, Pomerleau is behind both the Industrial Research Chair at École de technologie supérieure in Montreal and research currently underway at the University of British Columbia

Client benefits

High-quality project with optimized eco-energy and functional performances, delivered within budget and schedule with savings on construction costs and operations.

Trades benefits

Time savings during installation, no waste of materials, possibility for prefabrication and preassembly, and zero rework.

Client/Owner/Operator Benefits

  • Precise and rich model data that can be promptly transferred to management and maintenance systems
  • Savings gained from better maintenance and operation
  • Time saved (data input, information searches, equipment accessibility)
  • Better monitoring of maintenance works and occupied spaces
  • Model and documentation available for future renovations, additions, alternatives, etc.
  • Work history available

Pomerleau’s leadership in this field garnered the 2015 Best in BIM Award and the Innovation in BIM Award from CanBIM.

Model authoring

The intelligent digital model plays a major role in the BIM process. At Pomerleau, we can create or evolve models for all kinds of uses for conceptual design, estimating, or construction simulation. We can also create precise installation and fabrication models for various trades, ultimately leading to a building operations model.

Design optimization and conformity to the client’s requirements

Using unique digital tools developed in-house, Pomerleau collaborates with the architects and engineering consultants on design-build projects to ensure conformity to client requirements and to optimize the project’s overall performance.

Strategy and benefits

  • Build virtually before going to the construction site.
  • Beware of extras and cost overruns! BIM-VDC used in a collaborative and integrated manner, is an insurance policy for a top quality final product, all the while staying within budget and schedule.

Multidisciplinary coordination

  • Using the model as a reference for construction and installation.
  • No more rework and waste of materials and time! Precise coordination between all disciplines, by phase and zone, prior to construction. This way, the updated model serves as a source of information to ensure quality of execution.

Construction Simulation and Methods Optimization

Want to build your project in fast-track mode? By simulating the construction process (combining the 3D intelligent model and schedule), we can explore various alternatives and construction methods to find the most suitable and efficient strategy for undertaking your project. Buildings and civil works can benefit equally from this approach.

Laser Scanning

Whether for identifying the existing conditions or recording the already- finished building or infrastructure, laser scanning technology provides an unsurpassed combination of speed and precision. The point cloud obtained can be processed later to serve various purposes and uses.

Operation

See the video

Data for Building Operation

When Pomerleau finishes construction of a building, the BIM model serves as the as-built model and is rich in valuable up-to-date data. Taking it only one step further, this model can be a source of information for facilities management.  Furthermore, BIM-VDC and mobile technology aid in quality assurance and control as well as commissioning. For the latter, connection to an integrated database can be provided to the owner for use during building operation.

Possible uses of BIM data during building operations

BIM uses go even further. Data generated during design and construction can be used for building operations, specifically in the maintenance of equipment, space and asset management, systems control and performance tracking.
